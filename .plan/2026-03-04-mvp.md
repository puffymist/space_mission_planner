# Space Mission Planner -- Implementation Plan

## Context

Build a personal-use 2D space mission planner web app. The user wants to plan interplanetary (and inter-moon) missions by launching spacecraft, scheduling instantaneous delta-v maneuvers, and visualizing trajectories under realistic n-body gravity. The physics must support gravitational slingshots and stable L4/L5 orbits. All body orbits are assumed circular and coplanar.

## Stack

- **Vite + React + plain JavaScript** (simple, fast iteration)
- **Canvas 2D** for rendering (sufficient for ~16 bodies + spacecraft)
- **Zustand** for state management
- **Velocity Verlet** integrator (symplectic -- good energy conservation for L4/L5)
- Bodies on **prescribed circular orbits** (analytical), only spacecraft numerically integrated
- Reference epoch: **J2000.0** with approximate mean longitudes

## Bodies (16 total)

Sun, Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Moon (Earth), Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto (Jupiter), Titan (Saturn), Triton (Neptune, retrograde)

## Phase 4: Spacecraft & Trajectory Integration

**Build:** Gravity computation, Velocity Verlet integrator with adaptive time stepping, spacecraft launch from body orbit, trajectory precomputation and rendering.

**Files:** `physics/gravity.js`, `physics/integrator.js`, `physics/trajectory.js`, `state/useCraftStore.js`, `canvas/drawTrajectory.js`, `canvas/drawSpacecraft.js`, `utils/interpolate.js`, `components/SpacecraftPanel.jsx`

**Adaptive time step:** `dt = clamp(C * min_distance_to_any_body / speed, 1s, 3600s)`. Small steps near bodies (slingshot accuracy), large steps in deep space (performance).

**Point recording:** Store a point every ~3600s in deep space; every step when near a body. Keeps arrays at ~1000-10000 points per segment.

**Result:** Launch spacecraft from any body. Trajectory renders as a colored curve affected by all 16 bodies' gravity. Spacecraft follows trajectory during playback.

---

## Phase 5: Delta-V System & Direction Helpers

**Build:** Schedule delta-v events at specific epochs. Magnitude slider with adjustable fineness (1 m/s to 30 km/s). Direction helpers: prograde, retrograde, radial in/out, anticlockwise/clockwise relative to selected body. Maneuver nodes on trajectory. Live trajectory recomputation.

**Files:** `physics/deltaV.js`, `components/DeltaVPanel.jsx`, `canvas/drawHelpers.js`

**Direction math:**
- Prograde: `normalize(velocity)`
- Radial out from body: `normalize(pos - bodyPos)`
- Anticlockwise relative to body: `rotate(radialOut, +90deg)`

**Result:** Add a 3 km/s prograde burn at Earth -- orbit becomes elliptical. Chain multiple burns to plan complex missions. Trajectory updates in real time as user adjusts sliders.

---

## Phase 6: Mouseover, Ghosts & Tooltips

**Build:** Hovering a trajectory point shows the epoch and velocity tooltip, plus semi-transparent "ghost" positions of all bodies and spacecraft at that epoch. Hovering a body shows its info.

**Files:** `state/useUIStore.js`, `canvas/drawGhosts.js`, `components/TrajectoryInfo.jsx`, `components/BodyInfo.jsx`

**Result:** Hover over the spacecraft's Mars arrival point -- see ghost Mars right there confirming the intercept. See ghost Jupiter's position to verify a slingshot alignment.

---

## Phase 7: Hohmann & Bi-Elliptic Transfer Helpers

**Build:** Select departure/arrival bodies. Compute ideal phase angle, delta-v budget, transfer time. "Find Next Window" snaps epoch to optimal launch date. Preview transfer ellipse on canvas. "Apply" creates spacecraft with the two burns. Bi-elliptic option with intermediate radius slider.

**Files:** `physics/hohmann.js`, `components/HohmannPanel.jsx`

**Key math:**
- Transfer semi-major axis: `a_t = (r1 + r2) / 2`
- Departure dv: `sqrt(GM/r1) * (sqrt(2*r2/(r1+r2)) - 1)`
- Phase angle: `pi - (2pi/T2) * transferTime`
- Launch window: solve `phaseAngle(t) = required` for nearest `t`

**Result:** Select Earth->Mars, click "Find Next Window", preview the transfer ellipse, click "Apply". A spacecraft with two maneuver nodes appears following an approximate Hohmann path (with minor perturbations from other bodies).

---

## Phase 8: Import/Export & Polish

**Build:** Export spacecraft (initial state + delta-v events) as JSON. Import reconstructs trajectories. Keyboard shortcuts (Space=play/pause, +/-=zoom, 1-9=jump to planet, arrows=pan). Scale bar, grid, label improvements.

**Files:** `utils/exportFormat.js`, `components/ImportExport.jsx`

**Export format:** JSON with spacecraft initial state and delta-v events (NOT trajectory points -- those are recomputed on import since body positions are deterministic from epoch).

**Result:** Plan a full mission, export it, close the app, reimport -- identical mission reconstructed.

## Verification Plan

After each phase, verify by running the dev server (`npm run dev`) and testing interactively:

1. **Phase 2:** Zoom from Neptune's orbit down to Jupiter's moons -- all visible and correctly positioned
2. **Phase 3:** Play at 1 day/s -- inner planets visibly orbit; Moon orbits Earth; Triton orbits Neptune clockwise (retrograde)
3. **Phase 4:** Launch from Earth -- spacecraft should stay in ~1 AU orbit if no burns applied. Launch from Io -- spacecraft should be captured by Jupiter's gravity
4. **Phase 4 (L4/L5):** Place spacecraft at Earth's L4 point (60deg ahead, same radius, same velocity). Integrate for 10+ years. Should remain in tadpole orbit, not drift away
5. **Phase 5:** Apply ~3 km/s prograde burn from Earth orbit -- trajectory should reach roughly Mars distance
6. **Phase 7:** Earth-Mars Hohmann -- total dv should be ~5.6 km/s, transfer time ~259 days
7. **Phase 8:** Export, reimport, verify trajectories match
